# 1.0 INTRODUCTION

This Equipment and System Maintenance Procedure establishes the standardized process for maintaining all machinery, electrical systems, fabrication equipment, and critical infrastructure within LIMSL’s production and manufacturing facility. As a fabrication and engineering company, LIMSL operates a diverse range of equipment, including welding machines, CNC systems, material-forming machines, overhead cranes, air compressors, and electrical protection systems requiring consistent maintenance, inspection, calibration, and compliance oversight to ensure safe, reliable, and efficient operations.
This document is controlled under LIMSL’s Quality Management System, with ownership assigned to the Maintenance Department and oversight by the Quality Control Unit. It is subject to periodic review, updating, and approval through the company’s formal document control and revision management process to ensure alignment with operational needs, regulatory requirements, and continuous improvement objectives. 
Ensuring the integrity, reliability, and safety of these machines is essential for operational continuity, product quality, and compliance with international standards such as ISO 9001:2015, ASME VIII div I, and other application codes and standards.
This procedure outlines the standardized approach LIMSL follows for maintaining, inspecting, installing, monitoring, and supporting all equipment and systems within the facility.

# 2.0 PURPOSE

The purpose of this procedure is to define a consistent, comprehensive, and systematic approach to the maintenance, inspection, and calibration of LIMSL’s equipment and systems. It aims to ensure operational reliability, equipment safety, compliance with statutory and OEM requirements, and optimal performance of all machinery and electrical infrastructure. Additionally, this procedure establishes clear roles, responsibilities, and reporting mechanisms, integrates performance monitoring through key performance indicators (KPIs), and supports continuous improvement and effective resource planning across all maintenance activities. minimizing capital loss which comes in the form of cost of equipment replacement, man hour loss, and drop in production rate.

# 3.0 SCOPE

This procedure applies to all equipment, systems, and infrastructure within LIMSL’s production, fabrication, and office facilities. It covers machinery used in manufacturing and fabrication, including welding machines, CNC lathes and mills, press brakes, shears, rolling machines, band saws, grinders, beveling machines, cold cutters, pipe and column welding machines, and overhead cranes; as well as supporting systems such as air compressors, air conditioning units in offices, and low-voltage (LV) electrical wiring and distribution infrastructure within the buildings.
3.1 Equipment and Systems Covered

# 4.0 TERMS AND DEFINITIONS

Corrective Maintenance: Maintenance carried out to restore equipment or systems to operational condition following a failure or breakdown.

Non-Conformity Report (NCR): Documentation of deviations from required standards, specifications, or procedures that require corrective action.
HSE (Health, Safety, and Environment): Department or practices ensuring personnel safety, regulatory compliance, and environmental protection during operations and maintenance.
Document Control: Process of managing the creation, review, approval, distribution, and revision of documents to ensure accuracy and accessibility.

6.0 EXECUTION PROCESS
This section outlines the standardized process flow for all maintenance, calibration, installation, and inspection activities at LIMSL. It is designed to operate as an independent, auditable system that supports equipment reliability, safety, traceability, and operational efficiency.

## 6.1 Overview of the Maintenance Management Process

The maintenance management process at LIMSL ensures all equipment, systems, and supporting infrastructure operate safely, reliably, and efficiently. This process integrates scheduled maintenance, unscheduled repairs, condition-based interventions, inspections, and calibration activities into a structured workflow. The workflow also incorporates planning, execution, verification, and reporting steps, supported by clear roles, responsibilities, and documentation.
KPI Integration Point: At the overview level, the maintenance manager and quality team monitor aggregated KPIs such as overall equipment availability, maintenance compliance rates, and cost efficiency to evaluate the effectiveness of the maintenance program.

## 6.2 Preventive Maintenance Process (Scheduled Maintenance)

Preventive maintenance (scheduled maintenance) is performed to reduce the risk of equipment failure, prolong asset life, and ensure consistent production output. The process includes the following steps:
Annual Maintenance Planning
Develop a comprehensive annual maintenance plan for all equipment and systems, including machinery, overhead cranes, air compressors, HVAC units, and electrical infrastructure.
Define maintenance intervals based on OEM recommendations, regulatory requirements, operational criticality, and historical performance data.
Incorporate calibration schedules and inspection routines into the plan.
Weekly / Monthly Scheduling
Break down the annual plan into weekly and monthly schedules.
Assign maintenance tasks to qualified technicians, considering skillset, equipment criticality, and production schedules.
Communicate planned maintenance to production and operations teams to minimize disruption.
Execution of Scheduled Maintenance
Technicians perform maintenance tasks following standard operating procedures and safety requirements.
Use checklists and forms to ensure all tasks are completed thoroughly.
Operators assist with pre-maintenance preparations and post-maintenance validations as required.
Verification and Sign-Off
Supervisors inspect completed maintenance tasks for quality, safety, and compliance.
Verify proper completion of all checklist items and record findings in the maintenance log.
Obtain sign-off from the maintenance supervisor or manager to confirm completion.
KPI Integration Point:
Preventive Maintenance Compliance: Percentage of scheduled tasks completed on time.
Equipment Availability: Proportion of production time equipment is available and operational.
Maintenance Backlog: Number of outstanding maintenance tasks beyond scheduled completion.

## 6.3 Corrective Maintenance Process (Breakdown / Unscheduled Repairs)

Corrective maintenance addresses equipment or system failures that occur unexpectedly, restoring operational functionality as quickly and safely as possible. The process includes the following steps:
Fault Reporting and Maintenance Request
Equipment operators or personnel report faults using the Maintenance Request Form (MRF).
All reports include a description of the fault, equipment ID, location, and urgency level.
The Maintenance Supervisor logs and prioritizes requests in the maintenance tracking system.
Diagnosis and Troubleshooting
Maintenance technicians assess the fault, determine the root cause, and propose corrective action.not
Where required, technical support from OEMs or external contractors is engaged.
Safety measures, including Permit-to-Work (PTW) and Lockout-Tagout (LOTO), are applied before any work begins.
Repairs
Technicians perform repairs according to approved procedures and OEM specifications.
Any replacement parts used are recorded, and critical spares are verified for compatibility.
All work is documented in the maintenance log.
Testing and Return-to-Service
Repaired equipment is tested to ensure full operational functionality and safety.
Supervisors verify compliance with operational, calibration, and safety standards.
Equipment is formally released back to production with updated records.
KPI Integration Point:
Mean Time to Repair (MTTR): Average time taken to restore equipment after a failure.
Breakdown Frequency: Number of unscheduled failures per equipment per period.
Recurrence Rate: Number of repeat failures of the same equipment or fault type.

## 6.4 Predictive Maintenance Process (Condition-Based Maintenance)

Predictive maintenance uses data from equipment condition monitoring to anticipate failures and schedule interventions before unplanned downtime occurs. Steps include:
Condition Monitoring
Collect operational data such as vibration, temperature, pressure, hydraulic levels, lubrication status, and electrical parameters.
Use sensors, diagnostic tools, and operator feedback to monitor performance trends.
Trend Analysis
Analyze collected data to detect abnormal patterns or deviations from acceptable limits.
Compare readings against historical performance, OEM thresholds, and safety standards.
Early Intervention Triggers
Schedule maintenance or replacement activities when data indicates an impending failure or deviation from optimal performance.
Coordinate predictive maintenance tasks with production schedules to minimize disruption.
KPI Integration Point:
Mean Time Between Failures (MTBF): Average operating time between equipment failures.
Condition Monitoring Effectiveness: Percentage of detected anomalies that result in successful preventive interventions.

## 6.5 Calibration Process

Calibration ensures all measuring instruments, equipment, and critical machinery operate within specified tolerances, maintaining accuracy and compliance with OEM and regulatory standards.
Calibration Planning and Scheduling
Establish an annual calibration schedule for all measuring tools, welding machines, and overhead cranes.
Determine calibration intervals based on OEM recommendations, equipment criticality, and past performance data.
Include both internal and external calibration resources.
Calibration of Measuring Instruments
Verify and adjust instruments against known standards.
Document results, corrective actions, and deviations in calibration logs.
Tag calibrated instruments with certificates indicating the next calibration due date.
Calibration of Welding Machines
Check voltage output, gas flow, wire feed rates, and functional parameters.
Perform adjustments according to manufacturer specifications.
Record results and issue calibration certificates.
Pre-Mobilization Testing of Overhead Cranes
Conduct load tests, functional checks of limit switches, brakes, and wire ropes before operational use.
Ensure compliance with statutory and OEM safety standards.
Record findings and issue certification of readiness.
Calibration Certificates and Tagging
Maintain a centralized calibration register for all instruments and equipment.
Ensure all calibrated items are tagged, dated, and traceable.
Retain certificates as part of the maintenance record.
KPI Integration Point:
Calibration Compliance Rate: Percentage of equipment calibrated on schedule.
Out-of-Tolerance Failures: Number of instruments or machines found outside acceptable limits.

## 6.6 Inspection Process

The inspection process ensures equipment, systems, and infrastructure operate safely, reliably, and in compliance with standards.

6.6.1 Daily Operator Inspections
Operators complete standardized pre-use checklists before starting equipment.
Identify abnormalities, hazards, or operational inefficiencies.
Report findings to the maintenance team for immediate action.
KPI Integration Point: Daily Inspection Compliance Rate — percentage of daily checks completed as required.
6.6.2 Periodic Maintenance Inspections
Scheduled inspections on a weekly, monthly, and quarterly intervals for all equipment classes (welding machines, CNC machines, presses, compressors, HVAC units, cranes).
Verify lubrication, alignment, electrical connections, and operational parameters.
Record inspection results in maintenance logs.
KPI Integration Point: Inspection Findings Closure Rate — percentage of inspection issues resolved within defined timelines.
6.6.3 Electrical Safety Compliance Inspection
Electrical Safety Compliance Inspections, including earthing, lightning protection, surge protection devices, and statutory inspections of cranes and pressure systems, shall be coordinated and owned by the HSE Department. Maintenance shall provide technical support as required.
Perform earthing resistance tests to ensure safe grounding.
Inspect lightning protection systems for proper functioning.
Verify surge protection devices and electrical panels for integrity and compliance.
Document all findings and corrective actions.
KPI Integration Point: Electrical Safety Compliance Score — percentage of systems passing inspection without non-conformities.
6.6.4 Special Inspections
Crane Load Testing: Verify lifting capacity and operational safety according to statutory requirements.
Pressure System Inspections: Examine air compressors, hydraulic systems, and pressurized vessels.
Earthing Grid Assessment: Measure and confirm resistance values meet safety standards.
Structural/Safety Function Tests: Inspect critical supports, machine guards, and infrastructure components.
KPI Integration Point: Special Inspection Compliance Rate — percentage of special inspections completed on schedule with no major non-conformities.

## 6.7 Documentation and Record Control

Proper documentation ensures traceability, compliance, and accountability for all maintenance activities. All records are maintained in accordance with the company’s Quality Management System (QMS).
Maintenance Records
Include detailed logs of all preventive, corrective, and predictive maintenance activities.
Document work performed, parts replaced, technicians involved, and completion dates.
Inspection Registers
Maintain records of daily, periodic, electrical safety, and special inspections.
Include findings, corrective actions, and responsible personnel.
Calibration Certificates
Keep official calibration certificates for measuring instruments, welding machines, and overhead cranes.
Ensure certificates are traceable and updated according to the calibration schedule.
Asset History Logs
Maintain complete lifecycle history for all equipment, including installation, maintenance, inspection, calibration, and repairs.
Use logs to inform maintenance planning, budgeting, and reliability analysis.
Responsibility: Maintenance Supervisors ensure accurate entry of records; the Maintenance Manager and Quality Department review and audit documentation regularly.

## 6.8 Installation, Commissioning, and Handover Support

This process ensures new or relocated equipment is installed safely, commissioned effectively, and formally handed over for operational use.
Pre-Installation Assessment
Evaluate site readiness, power supply, and safety requirements.
Verify space, foundation, and structural integrity.
Supervision
Maintenance team oversees installation, ensuring compliance with OEM specifications and safety procedures.
Coordinate with production and operations to minimize disruption.
Commissioning Procedure
Conduct functional tests of all equipment, including safety devices, operational parameters, and performance verification.
Identify and resolve any installation or operational defects.
Handover Protocol
Issue a formal handover document confirming equipment readiness, safety compliance, and operational status.
Ensure operators and maintenance personnel are briefed on operational and maintenance requirements.
KPI Integration Point: Installation Defect Rate — number of installation defects per project, measured as a percentage of total installations.

## 6.10 Annual Maintenance Planning and Budgeting

Annual planning ensures that resources, spare parts, tools, and personnel are effectively allocated to meet maintenance objectives and operational requirements.
Budget Development
Prepare an annual maintenance budget aligned with the corporate financial plan.
Include costs for spare parts, consumables, contractors, training, calibration, and tools.
Submit budget for approval to Management and Finance.
Spare Parts Forecasting
Identify critical spare parts based on equipment criticality, historical failures, and OEM recommendations.
Plan inventory levels to minimize downtime while controlling capital expenditure.
Contractors and OEM Support
Identify external support requirements for specialized maintenance, calibration, or installation.
Schedule contractor activities in line with production and preventive maintenance schedules.
Tools, Training, and Resource Planning
Allocate necessary tools, equipment, and manpower for maintenance activities.
Include internal and external training plans to maintain staff competency.
KPI Integration Point:
Budget Adherence: Percentage of actual expenditure versus planned maintenance budget.
Annual Maintenance Cost Index: Maintenance cost per unit of production or asset value.

## 6.11 OEM Interfacing and Spare Parts Management

Proper interfacing with OEMs ensures technical compliance, timely support, and availability of authentic spare parts.
Warranty Support
Coordinate with OEMs for warranty claims and defect resolution during the Defect Liability Period (DLP).
Technical Escalation
Engage OEM experts for technical guidance on complex repairs, calibration, or equipment modifications.
Spare Parts Verification and Approval
Verify all spare parts against OEM specifications before use.
Maintain records of parts received, approved, and used for accountability.
KPI Integration Point:
OEM Response Time: Average time for OEM to respond to technical queries or support requests.
Spare Part Lead Time: Average time from order placement to receipt of approved spare parts.

## 6.12 Safety, Permit-to-Work, and Lockout–Tagout (LOTO)

Safety compliance is mandatory for all maintenance activities to protect personnel and equipment.
Safety Requirements Before Maintenance
Identify hazards associated with each maintenance task.
Ensure use of personal protective equipment (PPE) and adherence to HSE guidelines.
Permit Issuance
Issue formal Permit-to-Work (PTW) for high-risk maintenance, including electrical, confined space, or crane operations.
Ensure permits are reviewed and authorized by HSE or Maintenance Supervisor.
Isolation Procedures (LOTO)
De-energize equipment and secure isolation points before maintenance.
Tag and lock energy sources to prevent accidental operation.
Confirm isolation before starting work and remove locks only after verification of safe completion.

## 6.13 Continuous Improvement and Change Control

Continuous improvement ensures the maintenance process evolves to improve reliability, efficiency, and safety.
Corrective and Preventive Actions
Investigate root causes of failures, non-conformities, or repeated defects.
Implement actions to prevent recurrence and document outcomes.
Process Enhancement
Review workflows, procedures, and maintenance effectiveness.
Introduce improvements to optimize resource utilization, reduce downtime, and enhance safety.
Non-Conformities Management
Record, investigate, and close non-conformities in alignment with the QMS.
Use findings to update procedures, checklists, and training programs.
KPI Integration Point:
CAPA Closure Rate: Percentage of corrective and preventive actions completed on time.
Reduction in Recurring Failures: Decrease in repeat failures over a defined period.

# 7. Inspection & Special Inspections

Inspection processes ensure equipment and systems remain safe, compliant, and reliable throughout their operational lifecycle.
7.1 Routine Operator Checks
Operators perform daily pre-use checks using standardized checklists (Form G1).
Tasks include verifying machine functionality, safety devices, lubrication, and operational parameters.
Any deviations or anomalies are reported immediately to the Maintenance Team.
KPI Integration Point: Daily Inspection Compliance Rate — percentage of operators completing checks on schedule.

7.2 Monthly and Quarterly Inspections
Scheduled inspections performed by maintenance personnel for all equipment classes.
Activities include detailed verification of mechanical, electrical, and hydraulic systems, lubrication, wear, and operational accuracy.
Findings are recorded in inspection registers (Form G2) and reviewed for follow-up actions.
KPI Integration Point: Inspection Findings Closure Rate, percentage of identified issues addressed within the defined timeframe.

7.3 Electrical Safety Inspections
Verify earthing resistance, insulation integrity, and electrical panel safety.
Check lightning protection systems and surge protection devices.
Record non-conformities and ensure timely corrective action.
KPI Integration Point: Electrical Safety Compliance Score — proportion of systems meeting compliance standards.
7.4 Crane, Pressure Vessel, Load Testing, and Earthing Grid Inspections
Conduct load tests on overhead cranes and lifting equipment in accordance with statutory and OEM standards.
Inspect air compressors, pressure vessels, and hydraulic systems for safe operation.
Assess earthing grids, structural supports, and safety-critical components for integrity and compliance.
KPI Integration Point: Special Inspection Compliance Rate — percentage of scheduled special inspections completed with no major non-conformities.

7.5 Inspection Reporting & Escalation
All inspection results are documented in the maintenance system.
Critical or unsafe conditions are escalated immediately to the Maintenance Manager and HSE Department.
Reports include recommended corrective actions, responsible personnel, and timelines for resolution.

# 8. Calibration and Metrology

Calibration ensures measurement accuracy, traceability, and compliance with OEM and regulatory requirements.
8.1 Calibration Traceability
All calibration activities are traceable to recognized standards.
Calibration certificates must reference standard instruments, measurement uncertainty, and date of calibration.
8.2 Instrument Classification
Classify instruments and equipment based on criticality, accuracy requirements, and operational use (e.g., welding machines, CNC measurement tools, overhead crane load sensors).
8.3 Calibration Intervals & Criteria
Define calibration intervals based on OEM recommendations, historical performance, and usage frequency.
Equipment out-of-tolerance triggers immediate recalibration or maintenance intervention.
8.4 Internal vs. External Calibration
Internal calibration performed by trained maintenance personnel using certified standards.
External calibration conducted by accredited service providers or OEM technicians when specialized equipment or traceable certification is required.
8.5 Tagging and Calibration Records
Each calibrated instrument or equipment receives a tag indicating calibration date, next due date, and the responsible person.
Calibration records are maintained in a centralized database for traceability, audits, and compliance reviews (Form H).

KPI Integration Point:
Calibration Compliance Rate: Percentage of equipment calibrated on schedule.
Out-of-Tolerance Failures: Number of instruments found outside acceptable limits during routine checks or production use.

Maintain accurate records in the centralized inventory system.
KPI Integration Point:
Spare Part Lead Time — time from request to delivery of parts.
Stock Availability — percentage of critical spares in stock when required.

12.3 Cross-Functional Communication
Scheduled coordination meetings between Maintenance, Operations, HSE, Quality, Procurement, and OEMs.
Use centralized reporting tools and dashboards to track maintenance activities, KPIs, inspections, and incidents.
Escalate critical issues immediately to Maintenance Manager and HSE for resolution.

# 14. Equipment & Machine Key Performance Indicators (KPI Framework)

The Equipment KPI framework provides a structured approach to measure and monitor machine performance, maintenance effectiveness, and lifecycle management. These KPIs support decision-making for cost control, operational efficiency, and asset management.

14.1 Maintenance Effectiveness KPIs

| KPI | Definition | Formula / Measurement | Frequency | Responsible |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Preventive Maintenance Compliance | Percentage of scheduled maintenance tasks completed on time | (Completed Scheduled Tasks ÷ Total Scheduled Tasks) × 100 | Monthly | Maintenance Supervisor |
| Corrective Maintenance Completion | Percentage of unscheduled repair tasks completed within the target time | (Completed Corrective Tasks ÷ Total Reported Corrective Tasks) × 100 | Monthly | Maintenance Manager |
| Mean Time to Repair (MTTR) | Average time to restore a failed machine | Total Downtime ÷ Number of Breakdowns | Monthly | Maintenance Manager |
| Mean Time Between Failures (MTBF) | Average operational time between failures | Total Operating Hours ÷ Number of Failures | Monthly | Maintenance Supervisor |
| Recurring Failure Rate | Number of repeated failures of the same type per period | (Repeat Failures ÷ Total Failures) × 100 | Monthly | Maintenance Supervisor |

14.2 Equipment Utilization & Availability KPIs

| KPI | Definition | Formula / Measurement | Frequency | Responsible |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Equipment Availability | Percentage of time equipment is operational | (Operating Time ÷ Planned Production Time) × 100 | Daily / Weekly | Operations & Maintenance |
| Machine Utilization | Proportion of total capacity actually used | (Actual Production Hours ÷ Total Available Hours) × 100 | Weekly / Monthly | Operations |
| Downtime Frequency | Number of stoppages per period | Count of downtime events per machine | Monthly | Maintenance Supervisor |
| Planned vs Unplanned Downtime Ratio | Compare planned maintenance downtime to unplanned failures | Planned Downtime ÷ Unplanned Downtime | Monthly | Maintenance Manager |

14.3 Cost & Financial KPIs

| KPI | Definition | Formula / Measurement | Frequency | Responsible |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Maintenance Cost per Machine | Total maintenance cost per machine per period | Total Maintenance Cost ÷ Number of Machines | Monthly | Finance / Maintenance Manager |
| Cost per Operating Hour | Maintenance cost per hour of machine operation | Total Maintenance Cost ÷ Total Operating Hours | Monthly | Finance / Maintenance Manager |
| Spare Parts Cost Index | Proportion of total maintenance costs spent on parts | (Spare Parts Cost ÷ Total Maintenance Cost) × 100 | Monthly | Procurement / Maintenance |
| ROI per Machine | Revenue generated per machine vs maintenance cost | (Machine Revenue ÷ Total Maintenance Cost) × 100 | Quarterly | Operations / Finance |

14.4 Condition & Lifespan KPIs

| KPI | Definition | Formula / Measurement | Frequency | Responsible |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Remaining Useful Life (RUL) | Estimated remaining lifespan of equipment | Based on condition monitoring, historical data, and OEM specifications | Quarterly | Maintenance Engineer |
| Condition Monitoring Score | Aggregate index from vibration, temperature, lubrication, and electrical monitoring | Weighted scoring system from predictive maintenance data | Monthly | Maintenance Supervisor |
| Out-of-Tolerance Events | Number of equipment instances operating outside recommended parameters | Count of deviations per machine | Monthly | Maintenance Supervisor |
| Calibration Compliance | Percentage of equipment calibrated within schedule | (Calibrated Equipment ÷ Total Equipment Requiring Calibration) × 100 | Monthly | Maintenance Supervisor |

14.5 Safety & Compliance KPIs

| KPI | Definition | Formula / Measurement | Frequency | Responsible |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Electrical Safety Compliance | Percentage of electrical systems meeting safety standards | (Compliant Systems ÷ Total Systems Inspected) × 100 | Quarterly | HSE / Maintenance |
| Special Inspection Compliance | Percentage of special inspections completed on schedule | (Completed Special Inspections ÷ Total Scheduled) × 100 | Quarterly | Maintenance Supervisor |
| Incident Rate Related to Equipment | Number of safety incidents involving equipment | Count of incidents / period | Monthly | HSE |

14.6 Analytics & Reporting Use
Cost Control: Compare maintenance costs per machine with production revenue to identify underperforming assets.
Predictive Analytics: Use MTBF, condition scores, and downtime trends to forecast failures and optimize preventive interventions.
Asset Life Management: Use RUL and condition scores to plan replacements, CAPEX, and lifecycle investments.
Performance Benchmarking: Compare KPIs across machines, shifts, or departments to optimize utilization and identify bottlenecks.
Maintenance ROI: Link maintenance spend to machine uptime, production output, and revenue generation for strategic decisions.
